About Japan


日本: Nippon or Nihon

Japan is an island country in East Asia, located in the northwest Pacific Ocean. It is bordered on the west by the Sea of Japan, and extends from the Sea of Okhotsk in the north toward the East China Sea and Taiwan in the south. Part of the Ring of Fire, Japan spans an archipelago of 6852 islands covering 377,975 square kilometers; the five main islands are Hokkaido, Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu, and Okinawa. Tokyo is Japan's capital and largest city; other major cities include Yokohama, Osaka, Nagoya, Sapporo, Fukuoka, Kobe, and Kyoto.

The culture of Japan has changed greatly over the millennia, from the country's prehistoric Jōmon Period, to its contemporary modern culture, which absorbs influences from Asia and other regions of the world.

Japan's regions


Hokkaido

It's known for its volcanoes and natural hot springs.


Tohoku

It's known for its beautiful lakes and Hirosaki Castle.


Kanto

It's known for Japan's seat of government, Tokyo Skytree and the Shibuya Crossing


Chubu

It's known for its sake (an alcoholic beverage) and koshihikari rice.


Kansai

It's known for Osaka and Himeji castles and other historical places.


Chugoku

It's known for the city of Hiroshima; it was largely destroyed by an atomic bomb during World War II.


Shikoku

It's known for its 88-temple pilgrimage of temples and festivals.


Kyushu

It’s known for its active volcanoes and awsesome beaches.


Okinawa

It's known for being a place where people come for scuba diving and its unique cuisine.
